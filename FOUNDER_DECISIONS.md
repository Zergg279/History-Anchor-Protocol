# Founder decisions preserved in v1

- Bitcoin is the sole canonical publication and ordering layer.
- HAP is a protocol, not a hosted news product.
- No native token, staking, miner set, validator set, or truth vote.
- Pseudonymous authorship and direct publication remain permissionless.
- Batching is optional cost sharing, never authority.
- Original records are append-only; context is added through new signed records.
- Bitcoin proves publication and integrity, not arbitrary physical truth.
- Multiple keys do not prove independent witnesses.
- Large evidence remains off-chain and must be independently preserved.
- No website, repository, coordinator, archive, peer list, founder key, or identity issuer is mandatory.
- Responsible publication is a transparent optional client profile, not a base-validity rule.
- Anyone may publish; nobody is automatically entitled to amplification.
- Bitcoin contains opaque commitments, not readable allegations or evidence.
- HAP shapes good-faith infrastructure and cannot compel hostile publishers.
- Funding remains voluntary and external: the signed v1 release may fix a contribution address for authenticity, but no address has consensus effect, founder ownership power, premine, protocol royalty, paid truth status, or governance privilege.
