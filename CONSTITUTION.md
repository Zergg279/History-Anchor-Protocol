# History Anchor Protocol Constitution

These properties are non-negotiable. Convenience software, interfaces, and policy profiles may change; the base protocol must not silently weaken them.

1. **Bitcoin is the sole canonical publication and ordering authority.** HAP creates no competing blockchain, miner set, validator set, staking system, monetary policy, or required token.
2. **Permissionless pseudonymous authorship.** Anyone may generate a key and sign a record without registration, legal identity, or founder approval.
3. **Permissionless publication.** Anyone may pay for direct Bitcoin publication or use any independent batch coordinator. No coordinator is canonical.
4. **Independent verification.** Validity can be established with local HAP software and the verifier's own Bitcoin node.
5. **Append-only memory.** Originals are never edited. Attestations, disputes, corrections, subject responses, restrictions, adjudications, and analysis are new signed records linked to earlier records.
6. **Evidence, not omniscience.** HAP proves exact bytes, signatures, publication, ordering, and reproducible relationships. It must never misrepresent an arbitrary external-world claim as mathematical truth.
7. **No truth voting.** Founders, miners, token holders, institutions, analysts, identities, and majorities do not vote facts into existence.
8. **Protocol, not product.** No website, application, relay, coordinator, peer, archive, explorer, domain, repository, trademark, or implementation is required for validity or survival.
9. **Replaceable transport and storage.** Peers and archives move and preserve data; they never decide whether Bitcoin canonically published it.
10. **Single-copy recoverability.** One complete surviving lawful archive plus Bitcoin history can reconstruct and re-seed the surviving memory layer.
11. **Local lawful policy.** Base-protocol validity is distinct from what an operator retrieves, stores, indexes, displays, recommends, restricts, or removes.
12. **Permissionless publication is not automatic amplification.** Optional clients may apply transparent responsible-publication profiles, but those profiles cannot invalidate a valid Bitcoin publication.
13. **Permanent proof is not necessarily permanent readability.** Bitcoin stores only compact opaque commitments. Identifiable content, retrieval advertisements, and decryption/opening material remain off-chain and may be restricted or erased by compliant operators.
14. **Context is append-only and first-class.** Subject responses, disputes, corrections, restrictions, and adjudications must be independently preservable and verifiable.
15. **Minimal Bitcoin footprint.** Bitcoin stores compact commitments, not large evidence files or human-readable allegations.
16. **Open source and open specification.** Anyone may inspect, fork, redistribute, mirror, and independently implement the system.
17. **No hidden founder privilege.** No protocol key can rewrite records, revoke valid Bitcoin commitments, grant truth status, or block independent implementations.
18. **Algorithm agility and frozen interpretation.** Future migrations add versioned proofs and rules without silently changing the meaning of historical objects.
19. **Good-faith limitation.** HAP can shape conforming infrastructure. It cannot force hostile software, mirrors, or publishers to preserve context, honour restrictions, or stop distributing copied material.
20. **Funding cannot buy protocol authority.** Contributions, sponsorships, service fees, and grants remain external to validity. They grant no token, truth status, governance right, publication priority, or founder royalty.

Only claims whose proof every conforming verifier can reproduce may be described as cryptographically proven. Other statements remain explicitly classified as signed declarations, derived inferences, or external-world claims.
