# Legal and operator boundaries

This document describes the intended separation between the open protocol, the reference software, and services operated with that software. It is not legal advice.

## Source publication is not service operation

HAP is published as neutral, general-purpose open-source software and a public interoperability specification. The project does not provide a hosted relay, archive, search engine, newsroom, identity service, or content-moderation authority merely by publishing this repository.

A person or organisation that deploys HAP and controls access to a public relay, controls search or ranking, determines what personal data is processed, or stores and serves evidence may acquire separate legal responsibilities as that service's operator or data controller. Those responsibilities depend on jurisdiction, features, users, content, and actual control—not on the HAP name or licence.

## Operator responsibility

Before opening a service to public submissions, an operator should obtain jurisdiction-specific advice and assess at least:

- online-safety and illegal-content duties;
- child-safety and mandatory reporting duties;
- privacy, data-protection, restriction, and erasure requests;
- defamation, harassment, contempt, copyright, and confidentiality;
- terrorism and other prohibited-content laws;
- security, abuse reporting, complaints, logging, and incident response.

The reference safe-relay and responsible-publication profiles reduce risk but do not guarantee legal compliance. A pseudonym does not remove legal obligations or prevent identification through operational records.

## High-risk operating choices

Risk materially increases where an operator:

- accepts unrestricted anonymous public submissions;
- automatically retrieves or preserves arbitrary evidence bytes;
- runs a public search or recommendation interface;
- knowingly continues to serve unlawful material;
- omits disputes, corrections, restrictions, or subject responses;
- markets protocol validity or Bitcoin anchoring as proof that a claim is true;
- controls user funds, exchanges cryptoassets, or safeguards keys for users.

The official source release does none of those things by itself.

## Funding

The genesis Bitcoin address accepts voluntary support for open-source development. It does not transmit, exchange, mix, custody, or safeguard assets for users. Contributions create no token, equity, governance, publication, ranking, or validity rights. Recipients remain responsible for tax and accounting obligations applicable to them.

## No universal immunity

Open source, decentralisation, pseudonymity, and Bitcoin anchoring are not blanket legal immunities. HAP shapes good-faith protocol behaviour but cannot control hostile forks, external mirrors, screenshots, or non-conforming publishers.

The safest initial release model is source publication without personally operating an unrestricted public content service. Operators should launch on regtest or signet, keep raw evidence hosting disabled by default, and commission legal and security review before public deployment.

## Official reference points

Operators should check current guidance from their own regulator. Relevant UK starting points include Ofcom's Online Safety Act regulation checker and illegal-content guidance, the ICO's controller and data-protection guidance, and the National Crime Agency's CSEA reporting guidance.
